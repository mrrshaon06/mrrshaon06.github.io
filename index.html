Short Bio

Dr. Mohammad Razaur Shaon is a highly motivated transportation engineering researcher with extensive experience in applying statistical and machine learning techniques for complex transportation data analysis. His research focuses on connected and autonomous vehicles, crash analytics, and improving transportation safety and mobility.

Education

  • Ph.D. in Civil & Environmental Engineering, University of Wisconsin-Milwaukee (2019)
  • M.Sc. in Civil & Environmental Engineering, South Dakota State University (2015)
  • B.Sc. in Civil Engineering, Bangladesh University of Engineering and Technology (2012)

Research Interests

  • Highway Safety and Crash Modeling
  • Machine Learning in Transportation
  • Statistical and Econometric Modeling
  • Intelligent Transportation Systems
  • GIS and Spatial Data Analysis

Research Experience

  • Assistant Research Professor, University of Tennessee Knoxville (2024-Present)
  • Associate Research Scientist, University of Connecticut (2020-2024)
  • Postdoctoral Research Specialist, University of Arizona (2019-2020)
  • Graduate Research Assistant, University of Wisconsin-Milwaukee (2016-2019)

Publications

  • Luo, X., Li, X., Shaon, M.R.R., & Zhang, Y. (2023). Multi-lane-merging Strategy for Connected Automated Vehicles on Freeway Ramps. Transportmetrica B.
  • Cottam, A., Li, X., Shaon, M.R.R., & Wu, Y.J. (2023). Investigating the Impacts of E-scooters on a Bike-sharing System in Tucson, Arizona. International Journal of Sustainable Transportation.
